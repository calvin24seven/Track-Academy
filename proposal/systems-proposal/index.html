01 / Discovery

Twelve issues. One root cause.

Speaking with the receptionist at the sports centre uncovered the daily friction that parents, staff, and the system create. These aren't edge cases — they're the normal operating reality.

01 Data

Upshot makes receiving emails optional — so most customers don't have it ticked. No email means no communication. The database is full of contacts you can't reach.

02 Data migration

Customers migrated from a previous platform to Upshot without their email addresses. Those contacts exist but are unreachable — lost in transit between systems.

03 Manual outreach

The receptionist is manually going through each customer one by one — checking if they have an email, asking for consent, updating records. Hours of work that a system should do automatically.

04 Wrong names

The registration form is not clear that details should be for the child, not the adult. Parents enter their own names. The database is full of adult names where children's names should be.

05 No querying

No way to select or query who has data consent turned off in Upshot. You can't even find out how big the problem is, let alone fix it in bulk.

06 Too many fields

The form requires multiple submissions per child — a 20+ step form × 3 kids = 60+ fields to fill in. Parents give up. Staff field the calls instead.

07 No own database

Track Academy doesn't have its own database. All data lives in Upshot — you can't query it freely, export it flexibly, or build anything on top of it without Upshot's permission and API access.

08 Manual sign-in

Parents manually sign each child in by hand at the track — 6 fields per child. If a parent has 3 children, that's 18 fields filled in at the door, every session, on paper.

09 No welcome email

No welcome email is sent after registration. Parents don't know session times, what to bring, where to go, or who to ask for. First impressions are confusion at the sports centre door.

10 Manual billing

After registration, admin manually bills parents. Some pay, some don't. Universal Credit chasing happens ad-hoc and is sometimes left unverified. No automation, no enforcement, no visibility.

11 Trial bottleneck

Children get one free session — but there's no streamlined flow from trial → sign-up → payment. Each trial is an admin task instead of an automated funnel.

12 Bad WiFi

The CEO's rebuttal: "the WiFi at the sports centre is bad." Fair point — but it's a solvable engineering problem, not a reason to keep paper sign-in sheets.

These aren't separate problems. They all trace back to one thing: Track Academy doesn't own its data or its workflows. Upshot is a great operational tool, but it's not a registration platform, a billing system, a sign-in tool, or a communications engine. When you try to make it do all those things — and you don't have your own database — this is what happens.

04 / Registration

A registration form that knows the difference between a parent and a child.

The current form is the #1 source of bad data and abandoned sign-ups. The rebuild starts with one question: "Who is this for?"

Current form (Upshot)

One form for everyone — confusing and long

  • Doesn't clearly separate parent from child details
  • Parents enter their own name instead of child's
  • 50+ fields in one page — overwhelming
  • Asks about GCSE results for 8-year-olds
  • Multiple submissions needed per child (3 kids = 60+ fields)
  • Email consent is optional — most leave it unchecked
  • No save-and-come-back
  • No sibling autofill
  • No mobile optimisation
Proposed form (our system)

Child-first, parent-second. Clear, short, mobile-friendly.

  • "First, tell us about your child" — unmistakable header
  • Child's name, DOB, school year — 4 fields to start
  • Age-adaptive: only shows relevant fields (no GCSEs for 8-year-olds)
  • Then: "Now, tell us about yourself" — parent section clearly separated
  • "Register another child?" — copies parent details, only asks child info
  • Progress bar: "Step 1 of 5" — visually unintimidating
  • Mobile-first: large tap targets, one column, thumb-friendly
  • Inline validation: catches errors on blur, not on submit
  • Autosave: never lose progress
  • Email consent: clearly explained, prominently placed — not hidden
Upshot stays in the loop. All registration data saves to Track Academy's Supabase database. Subject to Upshot API access, we sync directly — or create a clean export/import workflow. Either way, Upshot gets the data it needs, and Track Academy gets a form that actually works.
05 / Sign-in

One scan. Tick the kids attending. Done.

Replace the 6-field-per-child paper sign-in with a QR code at the sports centre door. Parent scans, checks which children are here, submits. 10 seconds. That's it.

How it works

1. A laminated QR code is placed at the sports centre reception desk.

2. Parent arrives, opens their phone camera, scans the code.

3. A mobile web page opens — no app to install. It shows their children's names with checkboxes.

4. Parent ticks the child (or children) attending, taps "Sign in". Done.

5. Attendance is recorded in the database in real time. Staff see who's here on a simple dashboard.

Multiple children? All listed on one screen. One scan signs in all of them. No 18-field paper form for a family of three.

Parent scans → checks children → done

No app to install. Works on any smartphone. Works offline — syncs when WiFi returns.

🔧 "But the WiFi at the sports centre is bad" — here's how we solve that

Offline-first design. The sign-in page is a Progressive Web App (PWA). It loads once and works without internet. When a parent scans and submits, the attendance is saved locally on the device and queued. The moment any connectivity returns — even a flicker of 4G — it syncs to the database. No data lost. No parent waiting.

Fallback: If a parent genuinely has no smartphone, staff can sign them in from the staff dashboard on a tablet or laptop. Still one-click per child — still no paper.

4G backup: A cheap 4G dongle (£15/month) at the sports centre provides a backup connection independent of the building WiFi. The system switches automatically.

07 / Communications

Automated emails. SMS when needed. Consent you can query.

Right now, most parents can't be emailed — because Upshot made it optional and nobody ticked the box. We fix the data problem first, then build the comms engine on top.

Immediate fix: SMS campaign to collect emails

The problem: Hundreds of parents in the database with no email — or no email consent. You can't email them to ask for their email. Circular.

The fix: A one-time SMS campaign via Twilio. Text parents a short link to a simple form:

Example SMS:
"Hi [parent name], Track Academy here. We're updating our records so we can send you session times, event invites, and important updates. Please add your email here: [short link]. It takes 30 seconds. Thanks! — The Track Academy team"

The form captures: email address + consent to email + confirmation of child's name. Data goes straight into your Supabase database — not lost in Upshot's consent maze.

$

11 / Roadmap

What gets built — and when.

A phased approach. Each phase delivers something staff can actually use — no waiting months for value.

Phase 1A

Website rebuild + database foundation + registration

All core website pages live. Multi-step child-first registration wizard saving to Supabase. Upshot sync workflow. Sanity CMS for staff editing. GA4 + Search Console. 301 redirects. Free Cloudflare hosting. Delivers: a working website and registration form that captures clean data.

Phase 1B

SMS campaign + email collection + welcome emails

Twilio SMS campaign to recover missing emails from the existing Upshot database. Simple email-capture landing page. Welcome email sequence built (post-registration, pre-first-session, post-first-session). Consent tracking in your database. Delivers: reachable parents and automated onboarding.

Phase 2

QR code sign-in + attendance tracking

PWA sign-in page. QR code generated for the sports centre. Offline-first sync. Staff attendance dashboard. Automated attendance records in Supabase — ready to feed Upshot. Delivers: end of paper sign-in. Hours saved every week for Yvonne and the reception team.

Phase 3

Automated billing + Stripe + UC verification

Stripe integration for quarterly billing (£26/child). Automatic payment collection. Failed payment handling. UC evidence upload flow with staff verification dashboard. Payment status tied to QR sign-in (unpaid = no sign-in). Delivers: end of manual billing. Predictable cashflow.

Phase 4

Trial session funnel + staff dashboard

Short trial sign-up form. Automated trial → registration → payment pipeline. Trial conversion dashboard. Staff dashboard with search, attendance views, payment status, and quick actions. Delivers: a self-serve pipeline. Staff manage exceptions, not every step.

Phase 5

Portals + athlete passports + advanced reporting

Parent portal (view progress, pay, register siblings). School portal (Athlete Passports, termly reports). Funder dashboard (live impact data). Volunteer management. Spond replacement evaluation. Delivers: a complete digital ecosystem owned by Track Academy.

Phases 1A and 1B can be delivered in 6–8 weeks. Phases 2–5 are progressively built under the monthly retainer. Each phase is scoped and priced before work begins. No surprises.
