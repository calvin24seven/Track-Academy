# Brand Guidelines — Track Academy

*Version 1.0 · July 2026*
*Foundation for website rebuild and future digital transformation.*

---

## 1. Brand Purpose

**Track Academy uses athletics as the engine for profound social change.**

We are the only athletics charity in the country that blends on-track training with off-track education, mentoring and life skills. We are not a sports club. We are a launchpad.

Our purpose: to create a launchpad for young people to break down barriers, unlock their potential and thrive.

---

## 2. Brand Promise

Every young person who joins Track Academy gets three things:
1. **A coach** who sees their athletic potential
2. **A mentor** who sees their human potential
3. **A community** that sees their future

This is the 360-degree approach. It is not a programme — it is a promise.

## 4. Brand Vocabulary

### Words we own
- **Launchpad** — not "platform," not "pathway." A launchpad implies propulsion.
- **Bright Futures** — the tagline. Use as a sign-off, a section header, a fund ask.
- **Trusted adult** — the core concept behind mentoring. One trusted adult changes everything.
- **360-degree approach** — athletics + education + mentoring + life skills. Describe it as one system, not four programmes.
- **Student-athlete** — not "participant," not "child." Young people who are both learning and competing.
- **Sport for good** — broader movement language. Use in funder-facing context.
- **Brent** — say it. We are not "London-based." We are in Brent. NW10. Willesden.

### Words we avoid
- "Beneficiaries" (charity jargon — use "young people" or "student-athletes")
- "Users" or "customers" (they are young people, families, partners)
- "Clients" (schools are partners, not clients)
- "Disadvantaged" as a noun (say "facing barriers" or "under-resourced communities")
- "Hard to reach" (they're not hard to reach; we haven't reached them yet)
- "Life-changing" without evidence (follow with a stat or a name)

## 5. Logo Usage

### Current logo
- "Track Academy Bright Futures" wordmark with transparent background
- Two versions: standard (dark text) and white writing variant
- Files: `research/Track Academy/Assets/Website images and logos/Logos/`

### Clear space
- Minimum clear space = height of the "T" in "Track" on all sides
- Never place logo on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast

### Minimum sizes
- Digital: 120px wide minimum
- Print: 30mm wide minimum

### Colour variants
- Primary: Full colour on white/light backgrounds
- Reversed: White version on dark/brand-colour backgrounds
- Monochrome: Single colour where necessary (black or white only)

### Don'ts
- Don't stretch, distort, or rotate
- Don't recolour outside brand palette
- Don't add drop shadows or effects
- Don't place on backgrounds with insufficient contrast

## 7. Typography

### Primary typeface recommendations

| Role | Typeface | Fallback | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Headings** | **Bricolage Grotesque** or **Space Grotesk** | sans-serif | Modern, characterful, works for both sport and social cause. Slightly condensed feel without being aggressive. |
| **Body** | **Inter** | system-ui, sans-serif | Clean, highly legible at all sizes. Non-negotiable for accessibility. |
| **Stats/Numbers** | **Bricolage Grotesque** (bold) or **Space Grotesk** (bold) | sans-serif | Impact numbers should pop. Large, bold, orange. |
| **Quotes** | **Inter** (italic, medium) | sans-serif | Testimonials should feel human, not decorative. |

### Type scale (responsive, rem-based)

| Element | Mobile | Desktop |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | 2.25rem | 4rem |
| H1 | 1.75rem | 3rem |
| H2 | 1.5rem | 2.25rem |
| H3 | 1.25rem | 1.5rem |
| Body | 1rem | 1.125rem |
| Body small | 0.875rem | 0.875rem |
| Caption | 0.75rem | 0.75rem |
| Stat number | 2.5rem | 4.5rem |

### Typographic rules
- Headings: tight leading (1.1-1.2), slightly negative letter-spacing on large sizes
- Body: generous leading (1.6) for readability
- Line length: max 65-75 characters for body text
- Never use all-caps for body text; OK for labels, badges, stat captions
- Numbers in stats should be orange, large, bold — they are the proof

## 9. Imagery Direction

### Photography
- **Real, not stock.** Use actual sessions, real athletes, real coaches. The research folder has 20+ authentic photos.
- **Action over posed.** Mid-stride, mid-conversation, mid-laugh. Not lineup shots.
- **Faces visible.** Young people's faces tell the story. Ensure photo consent is cleared.
- **Diverse representation.** The community is majority Afro-Caribbean diaspora. The imagery should reflect that authentically, not as a diversity checklist.
- **Track as backdrop.** The red track, the green infield, the Willesden Sports Centre sign — these are location anchors.

### Image treatments
- Hero images: full-bleed, darkened gradient overlay for text legibility
- Cards: clean, no rounded treatments, full-bleed within container
- Quote cards: portrait crop with background blur
- Stats sections: no image; let the numbers speak

### What to avoid
- Stock photos of random athletes
- Overly filtered/edited photos
- Cheerful diverse group looking at camera (the charity cliché)
- Sad/hopeless "before" photos (we don't use poverty as a marketing tool)

## 11. Tone in Different Contexts

| Context | Tone shift |
|---|---|
| **Parent-facing** | Warmer, simpler, reassuring. You're speaking to a mum in Brent at 9pm on her phone. |
| **School-facing** | More structured, evidence-led. You're speaking to a Head of PE or SLT member. Reference Ofsted, Pupil Premium, Athlete Passports. |
| **Funder-facing** | Data-first, story-second. You're speaking to a grants officer at Sport England. Lead with stats and outcomes. |
| **Young person-facing** | Direct, energetic, inclusive. "All abilities. All welcome." |
| **Donate/Support** | Urgent but not desperate. Clear what £X does. Connect to a face/name. |

---

## 12. Do Not

- Do not use "life-changing" without evidence
- Do not use stock photos
- Do not use "beneficiaries" or "service users"
- Do not place orange text on white (fails contrast)
- Do not use Comic Sans, Papyrus, or any decorative font
- Do not use gradient backgrounds for body text
- Do not auto-play video or audio
- Do not use pop-ups for donations (immediate exit intent is OK, but don't interrupt)
