# Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs) — Track Academy

*Version 1.0 · July 2026*
*Three audiences. Three journeys. One website.*

---

## Master Principle

Track Academy's website must serve three distinct Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs) without making any of them feel like an afterthought. The homepage routes them. The navigation guides them. The content speaks to them directly.

The three ICPs are:
1. **Parents** — primary registration and revenue
2. **Schools** — partnership revenue and impact scale
3. **Funders** — credibility, grants, sustainability

A secondary audience exists — young people themselves — but they typically arrive through a parent or school. The site should still speak to them in certain contexts (events, competitions, performance coaching).

---

## ICP 1: Parents

### Profile
A parent or guardian in Brent or surrounding NW London boroughs, aged 28-55, likely Afro-Caribbean diaspora, may be on Universal Credit or other financial support. Their child is aged 5-21 and either loves sport, needs a safe space after school, or has been referred by a school or social service.

### Sub-segments

#### 1a. Fee-paying parent
- Can afford £25/term
- Looking for quality coaching + education support
- Values the community and mentorship
- Typically professional or working families in Brent

#### 1b. Subsidised/low-income parent
- On Universal Credit or financial support
- Sessions are free
- May feel stigma — the site must remove barriers, not add them
- May be referred by social services, school, or word of mouth
- Registration form complexity is currently a barrier

#### 1c. Parent of child with SEND
- Looking for inclusive, supportive sessions
- Needs reassurance about safeguarding and tailored support
- Matthew's Tuesday SEND sessions (5-6pm, ages 8-16) are the entry point
- Needs clear comms about what to expect

### What they want
1. **To know their child is safe** — safeguarding visible, DBS-checked staff, clear policies
2. **To know if they can afford it** — £25/term or free if on support, stated clearly
3. **To know when and where** — Willesden Sports Centre, Tue/Thu/Sat, specific times
4. **To register easily** — current form is too long and confusing
5. **To understand what their child gets** — coaching + education + mentoring + life skills, not just athletics
6. **To see other parents' experiences** — testimonials, faces like theirs

### What they worry about
- "Is it really only £25 a term?" (yes — make this clear)
- "Is my child safe there?" (yes — safeguarding page)
- "My child has autism — can they come?" (yes — SEND sessions)
- "Will my child be the only one who looks like them?" (no — 85% from ethnic minorities, Brent-rooted)
- "I'm on Universal Credit — does that matter?" (no — you attend free, no stigma)
- "The form is asking about my child's GCSE results but they're 8 years old" (this is a current bug in the Upshot form)

### What they do NOT care about
- The charity number
- The trustee board composition
- England Athletics affiliation (unless their child wants to compete)
- The WordPress plugin update history

### Journey

#### Step 1: Discovery
- Google search: "athletics for kids Brent", "after school sports Willesden", "youth club NW10"
- Or: referred by school, social services, friend, or sees an Instagram post
- Or: sees a flyer at Willesden Sports Centre

#### Step 2: Landing
- Arrives on homepage
- Sees hero: "Changing lives since 2007"
- Sees CTA: "Register your child"
- Scrolls: sees the 3-1-1 audience routing ("I'm a parent / I'm from a school / I'm a funder")

#### Step 3: Parent route
- Clicks "I'm a parent" or "Register your child"
- Lands on a parent-focused page with:
  - Session times and ages
  - £25/term or free message
  - What's included (athletics + education + mentoring + life skills)
  - Safeguarding reassurance
  - Photos of real sessions
  - Parent testimonial
  - Simple "Register your child" CTA

#### Step 4: Registration
- Opens the multi-step registration wizard
- Step 1: Child details (name, DOB, age group)
- Step 2: Parent/contact details
- Step 3: Medical and emergency contact
- Step 4: Consent and confirmation
- Save progress / come back later
- Sibling autofill if registering more than one child
- Clear progress indicator
- Confirmation email with what to bring, when to arrive, where to go

#### Step 5: Post-registration
- Email confirmation with:
  - Session details
  - What to wear/bring
  - How to pay (or confirmation of free place)
  - Spond invite link
  - Contact for questions
- Automated reminder before first session
- Welcome at the track desk

## ICP 2: Schools

### Profile
A Head of PE, Deputy Head, Pupil Premium Coordinator, or MAT executive in Brent and surrounding NW London boroughs. They have Pupil Premium funding to spend on personal development, and they need evidence for Ofsted that their interventions are working.

### Sub-segments

#### 2a. Primary school
- PE & Sport Premium to spend
- Needs after-school clubs, CPD for teachers
- Wants movement literacy → event skills progression
- Safeguarding is a prerequisite, not a differentiator

#### 2b. Secondary school
- Pupil Premium funding
- Needs character & leadership, attendance, behaviour, mentoring
- Wants Ofsted Personal Development evidence
- May want competition pathways and talent ID

#### 2c. MAT / cluster
- Multiple schools
- Needs standardised reporting across sites
- Wants branded partnership recognition
- Bulk pricing expected

### What they want
1. **Ofsted-ready evidence** — Athlete Passports tracking attendance, confidence, fitness, behaviour
2. **Termly impact reports** — ready for SLT, Ofsted, governors
3. **Admin-light** — they don't want to organise it; they approve the pupil list and Track Academy does the rest
4. **Pupil Premium justification** — clear outcomes for the pupils they're spending PP on
5. **Safeguarding** — DBS-checked, insured, risk assessments provided
6. **Clear pricing** — Bronze/Silver/Gold, know what you get at each tier
7. **A relationship** — someone who shows up, delivers, and communicates

### What they worry about
- "Will this be another thing my staff have to manage?" (no — admin-light, Track Academy schedules and delivers)
- "Can I justify this to my Head/governors/Ofsted?" (yes — termly impact report, Athlete Passports)
- "Is Pupil Premium eligible?" (yes — fully funded for PP pupils)
- "Are the coaches qualified and safe?" (yes — DBS-checked, insured, England Athletics standards)
- "What does it actually cost?" (clear tiers — £1,950/£3,950/£6,950)
- "Will our pupils actually engage?" (yes — 98% recommendation rate, 94% believe they can achieve their goals)

### Journey

#### Step 1: Discovery
- Search: "school sports partnership Brent", "Pupil Premium athletics", "after school sports clubs NW London"
- Or: referral from another school (Kayla Anderson at St Claudine's, Mike Finnie at Newman Catholic College)
- Or: sees Track Academy at a community event
- Or: Track Academy outreach to school

#### Step 2: Landing
- Arrives on homepage or schools page directly
- Sees "Partner with Track Academy" CTA
- Sees school partner testimonial quote immediately

#### Step 3: Schools route
- Clicks "I'm from a school" or goes directly to /schools
- Lands on schools page:
  - "Bright Futures Through Sport — Pathways to Excellence"
  - Why schools partner (Character & Leadership, Inclusion & Access, Pathways to Excellence)
  - What we deliver (Primary & Secondary)
  - Funding model (Pupil Premium / PE & Sport Premium)
  - Why this passes the SLT test
  - How it works (3 steps)
  - Testimonials from Kayla Anderson and Mike Finnie
  - Impact stats (66% improved school performance, 87% skills, 94% believe goals, 89% community)
  - Partnership tiers (Bronze/Silver/Gold with pricing)
  - CTA: "Become a Partner School"

#### Step 4: Enquiry
- Clicks "Become a Partner School" or "Talk to us"
- Short form: School name, role, email, phone, pupil numbers, preferred programme
- Or books a call directly

#### Step 5: Proposal conversation
- Track Academy CEO/staff follow up with a tailored proposal
- Pilot term agreed
- Pupil list shared
- Schedule confirmed

#### Step 6: Delivery & reporting
- Sessions delivered on-site or at Willesden
- Athlete Passport data captured weekly
- Termly impact report delivered
- Relationship renewed

## ICP 3: Funders

### Profile
A grants officer, corporate social responsibility manager, or individual major donor evaluating Track Academy for funding. They see dozens of applications per week. They need to see impact, governance, and fit — fast.

### Sub-segments

#### 3a. Statutory funders
- Sport England, National Lottery, local authority (Brent Council)
- Want: alignment with strategy, measurable outcomes, safeguarding, value for money
- Application process: formal, evidence-heavy

#### 3b. Trusts and foundations
- Laureus, Sported, Jack Petchey, Comic Relief, BBC Children in Need
- Want: story + data, community reach, innovation, sustainability
- Relationship: ongoing, reporting-heavy

#### 3c. Corporate sponsors
- Local businesses, national brands (like Tenzing, Reliance Bank)
- Want: brand visibility, community goodwill, employee engagement
- Relationship: transactional but can deepen

#### 3d. Individual donors
- via Whydonate, direct donations, fundraising events (12-Hour Relay)
- Want: emotional connection, see where their money goes, easy give
- Relationship: light-touch, recurring if nurtured

### What they want
1. **Evidence of impact** — real numbers, outcome data, demographics
2. **Governance** — charity status, trustees, safeguarding, financial stability
3. **Reach** — who they're reaching (Brent, under-resourced communities, 85% ethnic minorities)
4. **Innovation** — what's different about Track Academy (360-degree model, only athletics charity with education + mentoring)
5. **Sustainability** — diversified income, not dependent on one funder
6. **Story** — a face, a name, a young person whose life changed
7. **Easy give** — clear donate path, suggested amounts

### What they worry about
- "Is this a one-person charity?" (no — 19+ employees, 9 trustees, established 2007)
- "Will my money actually reach the young people?" (yes — London Living Wage Employer, low overhead)
- "Can they report back to me?" (yes — they already produce annual reports, termly school reports)
- "Are they financially stable?" (yes — Connie left it in the "most stable position ever")
- "Do they have a future plan?" (yes — 2025 priorities including this very website refresh)

### Journey

#### Step 1: Discovery
- Referral from another funder (Laureus → Sported)
- Or: Track Academy approach (grant application)
- Or: sees media coverage (Runner's World feature, LinkedIn post)
- Or: sees the website after an introduction

#### Step 2: Landing
- Arrives on homepage
- Sees hero: mission, energy, real young people
- Scrolls: sees impact stat band immediately
- Sees "I'm a funder" audience route

#### Step 3: Funder route
- Clicks "I'm a funder" or goes to /impact
- Lands on a dedicated impact page:
  - Headline impact numbers (577 sessions, 331 young people, 98% recommendation)
  - Outcome data (66% improved school performance, 94% believe goals, etc.)
  - Demographics (85% ethnic minorities, 64% girls, 85% from Brent)
  - Context (41% Brent child poverty, 28% feel safe spaces)
  - Stories (Jack Petchey winners with names and outcomes)
  - Annual report download
  - Funder logos already supporting us
  - CTA: "Support our work" or "Talk to us about funding"

#### Step 4: Contact / download
- Downloads annual report PDF
- Or fills short contact form: "I'm from [organisation], I'd like to discuss funding"
- Or clicks through to Whydonate for a direct donation

#### Step 5: Due diligence
- Reviews annual report
- Checks Charity Commission (1164222)
- Reviews safeguarding policies
- Talks to Patrik / Frederik
- Funding decision made

#### Step 6: Partnership
- Grant agreement signed
- Reporting schedule agreed
- Impact data delivered (increasingly from our own database)
- Renewal

## Secondary Audience: Young People

### Profile
Aged 5-21, currently registered or potential. They come to the site through a parent, school, or social media. They want to know: what's happening, when, and can they be part of it.

### What they care about
- Events and competitions
- Performance coaching (if ambitious)
- Session times
- Photos they might be in
- The Instagram feed

### Where they go
- /events
- /competitions-fixtures
- /homepage/performance-coaching
- Instagram (not the website, mostly)

### How the site serves them
- Events page should be visual, energetic, shareable
- Competition fixtures should be clear, filterable, with entry links
- Performance coaching should feel aspirational but accessible
- The Instagram feed on the homepage serves as their content
