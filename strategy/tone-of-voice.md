# Tone of Voice — Track Academy

*Version 1.0 · July 2026*
*Derived from analysis of 10+ real Instagram captions, 5 LinkedIn posts, and the Annual Report 2024.*

---

## 1. The Voice in One Sentence

Track Academy speaks like a trusted coach at the track edge: warm, direct, community-rooted, and quietly confident. We celebrate without bragging. We believe without preaching. We credit the team before ourselves.

## 2. Voice Principles

### Principle 1: Human first, formal never

| Don't write | Write |
|---|---|
| "We deliver programmes to beneficiaries" | "We're here for young people" |
| "Utilising athletics as a vehicle" | "We use athletics" |
| "We are pleased to announce" | "We're buzzing to share" |
| "In the previous reporting period" | "This year" |

**Rule:** If you wouldn't say it out loud to a parent at the track desk, don't write it on the website.

### Principle 2: Short sentences. Fragments for emphasis.

From their real posts:
> "What a time. Our 2026 Outdoor Open Meet was about more than fast times and PBs. It was about creating opportunities."

> "That's Track Academy. Not just PBs. A launchpad."

**Rule:** Vary rhythm. Long sentence gives context. Short sentence lands the point. Shorter still when it matters.

### Principle 3: Credit the team. Always.

From every real post:
> "A huge thank you to every athlete, coach, official, volunteer, parent and supporter who made the evening possible."

> "All credit to our coaches, mentors, tutors and staff team who show up every session every week."

**Rule:** Nothing is achieved alone. When celebrating, name names. When thanking, be specific.

### Principle 4: Show, don't tell.

| Don't write | Write |
|---|---|
| "We are inclusive" | "All abilities. All welcome." |
| "We have strong outcomes" | "98% of our athletes would recommend Track Academy to a friend." |
| "We help young people" | "We are the only athletics charity in the country that also offers education, mentoring and life-social skills sessions." |

**Rule:** A number beats an adjective. A name beats a number. A story beats a name.

### Principle 5: Brent, not London.

| Don't write | Write |
|---|---|
| "London-based charity" | "Based in Brent, NW10" |
| "Young people in the capital" | "Young people in Brent" |
| "Willesden Sports Centre, London" | "Willesden Sports Centre, NW10 3QX" |

**Rule:** We are specific because our community is specific. Brent has 41% child poverty. Saying "London" dilutes the urgency.

### Principle 6: Emotion without saccharine.

From their real posts:
> "Her teacher called us shocked — said she's 'a different child.' At home, she's happier. She even cooks dinner now to 'train like an athlete.'"

> "He believes in them before they believe in themselves."

**Rule:** We let the young person or parent speak. We don't editorialise over their words. The quote does the work.

### Principle 7: Emoji — moderate, intentional.

Pattern observed in real posts:
- Used for emphasis at the start or end of a post
- Used for structured info (schedule blocks: 📍 📅 ⏰ 👦 💰)
- Brand emoji: 💙🧡 (the brand colours), 🏃 (athletics), 🔥 (energy), 🏅 (achievement)
- Never used mid-sentence
- Never emoji-only as a reaction

**Rule for the website:** Use sparingly. Hero CTAs don't need emoji. Social feeds and event listings can use them. Impact stats can have ONE small emoji next to the number at most.

## 3. Vocabulary Guide

### Words we use

| Word | Why |
|---|---|
| Launchpad | Our core metaphor. A launchpad doesn't just support — it propels. |
| Bright Futures | Our tagline. Used in logo, sign-offs, fund asks. |
| Student-athlete | Not "participant." They are both learning and competing. |
| Trusted adult | Our mentoring concept. Research-backed. One trusted adult changes everything. |
| 360-degree approach | Our unique model. Athletics + education + mentoring + life skills. |
| Brent | Say it. We belong here. |
| Willesden | Our home. The sports centre. The track. NW10 3QX. |
| Thrive | Not "survive." We believe in thriving, not just surviving. |
| Family | How we describe the community. Safe, supportive, belonging. |
| Show up | What our staff do. Every session, every week. |
| Break down barriers | What we do. Not "overcome" (passive). We break them down (active). |
| Launchpad for | The construction. "A launchpad for young people to break down barriers, unlock their potential and thrive." |

### Words we don't use

| Word | Why not | Say instead |
|---|---|---|
| Beneficiaries | Charity jargon | Young people, student-athletes |
| Service users | Clinical | Young people, families |
| Clients | Transactional | Partners (schools), funders, supporters |
| Hard to reach | They're not hard to reach; we haven't reached them | Under-resourced communities |
| Disadvantaged (as noun) | Labels people by what they lack | Facing barriers, under-resourced |
| Beneficial | Bureaucratic | Helpful, transformative |
| Stakeholder | Corporate | Partners, community, funders |
| Synergy | Nonsense word | (delete it) |
| Utilise | Pretentious | Use |
| Empower | Overused and patronising | Equip, support, give tools, stand with |
| Needy | Patronising | (delete it) |
| Life-changing (without evidence) | Empty | Follow with: a stat, a name, a story |
| Passionate | Empty | Show passion through action and specificity |

## 5. Microcopy

### Buttons
- Register your child (not "Sign up now" or "Enrol here")
- Partner with us (schools)
- Support our work (donate/volunteer)
- See our impact (not "Learn more" — be specific)
- Enter on OpenTrack (competition entries)
- Join our Spond (member communication)
- Talk to us (not "Contact us" — warmer)

### Form labels
- "Your child's name" (not "First name of participant")
- "Your name" (not "Contact first name")
- "How can we help?" (not "Message")
- "Date of birth" (not "DOB")

### Error states
- "We need this to register your child" (not "This field is required")
- "Please check your email address" (not "Invalid email format")

### Empty states
- "No events coming up right now. Check back soon or follow us on Instagram."
- "We haven't published fixtures for this period yet. Join our Spond to get notified."

### Loading states
- "Finding events near you..."
- "Saving your registration..."

## 6. Writing for SEO

### Headline pattern
- Leading with the topic: "Athletics in Brent for young people aged 5-21"
- Not keyword-stuffed: "Athletics Brent London Youth Sports Club Charity Track Academy"
- H2s answer questions: "How much does Track Academy cost?", "What age groups do you coach?", "How do I register my child?"

### Meta description formula
"[Service]. [Audience]. [Location]. [Key differentiator]."

Example: "Athletics, education and mentoring for young people aged 5-21 in Brent. The only athletics charity in the country offering 360-degree support. £25 per term. Register your child."

### Page title formula
"Track Academy — [Page purpose] | Athletics in Brent"

Examples:
- "Track Academy — About Us | Athletics in Brent"
- "Track Academy — Schools Partnership | PE & Pupil Premium in Brent"
- "Track Academy — Impact | 331 young people, 577 sessions"

## 7. Things to Never Write

- "We are passionate about..." (show it, don't say it)
- "Thanks for your interest in Track Academy" (plane ticket energy)
- "Lorem ipsum" (this was on their current contact page)
- "We touch the lives of..." (cliché)
- "Making a difference" (unquantified — follow with a stat or delete)
- "Empowering disadvantaged youth" (every charity says this and it means nothing)
- "At the end of the day..." (say what you mean)
