## Journey 1: Parent — From Discovery to Registered Child

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger:** Child shows interest in athletics OR parent looking for after-school activities OR school referral OR sees Track Academy content on Instagram or local community group.

**Action:** Parent searches Google or sees social media post.

**Where they land:** Homepage (`/`) or specific programme page (if search result).

**What the site must do:**
- Within 5 seconds: parent knows this is an athletics charity in Brent, for ages 5-21, and it's affordable (£25/term or free)
- Hero CTAs visible above the fold: "Register your child"
- Image: real young people, not stock
- Social proof: 331 young people, 17 years

### Stage 2: Evaluation
**Trigger:** Parent wants more info before registering.

**Action:** Parent navigates to:
1. `/athletics` — session times, ages, pricing
2. `/safeguarding` — safety reassurance
3. (Optional) `/education` — what else their child gets
4. (Optional) `/impact` — is this actually any good?

**Where they go next:** "Register your child" CTA on any of these pages.

**What the site must do:**
- Pricing is clear: £25/term or free if on financial support. No hidden costs.
- Session times visible upfront
- Safeguarding prominent — DSL names, DBS-checked, England Athletics
- testimonials from other parents visible on impact page
- The "only athletics charity to also offer education and mentoring" message reinforces value beyond sport

### Stage 3: Registration
**Trigger:** Parent decides to register.

**Action:** Parent clicks "Register your child" CTA → lands on `/register`

**What the site must do:**
- Multi-step wizard is visually clean and unintimidating
- Step 1: Child details (simple: name, DOB) — not a 50-field wall
- Progress bar visible — "Step 1 of 7"
- Parent can save and come back later (doesn't lose progress)
- Sibling autofill after first child registered
- Clear path for low-income (Universal Credit) parents — fee waiver is automatic, not stigmatised
- On submission: parent sees confirmation, what to expect, when to come
- Email confirmation with session details, Spond invite, what to bring

### Stage 4: First Visit
**Trigger:John parent receives confirmation email.

**Action:** Parent brings child to Willesden Sports Centre at the right time.

**What the site must do (post-visit):**
- Parent joins Spond via link in confirmation email
- Parent receives reminder 24 hours before first session (Spond automation)
- Parent meets staff at track desk who know their child's name and needs
- Post-session follow-up (Spond or email): "How did it go?"

### Stage 5: Ongoing Engagement
**Trigger:** Child attends regularly.

**Action:** Parent uses Spond for session info, event invitations, fee payments.

**What the site does (supporting role):**
- Parent can return to website for events (`/events`)
- Parent can share impact stories on social media (organic advocates)
- Parent can introduce other families (referral loop — website should make sharing easy)

### Stage 6: Advocacy
**Trigger:** Child has positive experience, parent becomes an advocate.

**Action:** Parent speaks to other parents, posts on social, attends Open Meet.

**What the site does:**
- Instagram embed on homepage keeps showing current energy
- Events page clear for parent to invite others
- "98% of our athletes would recommend Track Academy to a friend" stat boosts confidence

## Journey 2: School — From Discovery to Partnership Signed

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger:** Head of PE or Pupil Premium co-ordinator needs an intervention for autumn term OR received Track Academy outreach OR heard from colleague at another school (Kayla Anderson at St Claudine's, Mike Finnie at Newman Catholic College).

**Action:** Searches Google for "school sports partnership Brent" or "Pupil Premium athletics" or types trackacademy.co.uk from referral.

**Where they land:** Homepage → "I'm from a school" audience router → `/schools`

### Stage 2: Evaluation
**Trigger:** School user exploring whether Track Academy is a fit.

**Action:** School user navigates the schools page:
- Reads the 3 pillars (Character, Inclusion, Pathways)
- Reads "What we deliver"
- Reads "How this passes the SLT test" — looking for Ofsted-ready answer
- Reads testimonials from St Claudine's and Newman Catholic College
- Reads the pricing tiers
- Reads safeguarding policies

**Where they go next:** May also check:
- `/impact` to validate the 66%/87%/94%/89% stats
- `/safeguarding` for compliance check
- `/team` to see who's involved

**What the site must do:**
- Make Ofsted relevance explicit: "Termly impact reports ready for SLT and Ofsted"
- Make Pupil Premium explicit: "All Pupil Premium pupils free"
- Make admin ease explicit: "We schedule, deliver and collect data — you approve the pupil list"
- Show case studies from named schools
- Pricing is transparent (Bronze/Silver/Gold)

### Stage 3: Enquiry
**Trigger:** School decides to explore further.

**Action:** School user clicks "Become a Partner School" or "Talk to us" → fills enquiry form on `/schools` page or `/contact`.

**Form fields:** School name, role, school type, approx Pupil Premium numbers, preferred programme, email/phone

**Submit routes to:** Patrik (CEO) or Erick (Education & Mentoring Co-ordinator)

### Stage 4: Proposal Conversation
**Trigger:** Track Academy responds to enquiry within 1-2 working days.

**Action:** Phone call or meeting between school and Patrik/Erick.
- Tailored proposal based on school's needs
- Pupil list agreed
- Schedule confirmed
- Tier selected

### Stage 5: Delivery Onboarding
**Trigger:** Partnership agreement signed.

**Action:** School provides pupil list; Track Academy schedules sessions.

**What the system needs to support:**
- School portal (Phase 2): upload pupil list, see Athlete Passports, download termly reports
- For MVP: admin handles via email/Google Docs

### Stage 6: Ongoing Partnership
**Trigger:** Sessions running.

**Action:** School receives termly impact report from Track Academy.

**What the site does (in support):**
- Schools page keeps showing current impact stats (potential for live data)
- Schools page showcases testimonials (potential for "our partner schools" section with logos)
- Annual report download available for SLT/governor review

### Stage 7: Renewal
**Trigger:** End of academic year, partnership up for renewal.

**Action:** School reviews impact report, decides to continue or upgrade tier.

**What Track Academy does:** Sends report via email (MVP) or school portal (Phase 2).

## Journey 3: Funder — From Discovery to Funding Decision

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger:** Track Academy applies for a grant OR funder receives a referral OR funder sees media coverage (Runner's World feature) OR funder sees a LinkedIn post.

**Action:** Funder searches Track Academy, lands on homepage.

### Stage 2: Evaluation
**Trigger:** Funder wants to validate Track Academy as a credible, impactful charity.

**Action:** Funder navigates to:
1. `/impact` — looks at stats, demographics, outcomes
2. `/about` — checks team, history, governance
3. `/safeguarding` — checks charity is safe
4. Returns to homepage for big picture

**What the site must do:**
- Homepage must immediately establish Track Academy's seriousness (impact stat band)
- Impact page must lead with numbers, then human stories
- Demographic data must be visible (85% ethnic minorities, 64% girls, Brent-rooted)
- pdf annual report download prominent
- Trust strip: England Athletics, London Living Wage, charity number
- "The only athletics charity in the country to also offer education, mentoring and life-social skills"

### Stage 3: Contact
**Trigger:** Funder decides to explore funding.

**Action:** Funder either:
- Downloads annual report PDF (lead capture optional)
- Fills "I'm a funder" form on `/support` or `/contact`
- Emails Patrik directly (email visible in footer)

**Form fields:** Organisation, your name, your role, email, funding interest (grants, sponsorship, partnership)

**Submit routes to:** Patrik (CEO)

### Stage 4: Due Diligence
**Trigger:** Track Academy responds to funder enquiry.

**Action:** Funder reviews:
- Annual report PDF
- Charity Commission record (link from footer)
- Safeguarding policies
- Financial history (from annual report)
- Team credentials

**What the site does:**
- All materials easily accessible
- Annual report is comprehensive
- Trust signals prominent
- No broken links or "Lorem ipsum"

### Stage 5: Funding Decision
**Trigger:** Funder completes internal review.

**Action:** Funder makes decision — yes, no, or request more info.

### Stage 6: Partnership
**Trigger:** Funding agreed.

**Action:** Grant agreement signed, reporting schedule confirmed.

### Stage 7: Reporting
**Trigger:** Track Academy delivers interim/final report to funder.

**What the system will do (Phase 2+):**
- Pull live data from Supabase
- Generate custom report with attendance, outcomes, demographic breakdown
- funder portal for ongoing visibility (future)

**MVP:** Manual report from annual report + Upshot data exports.

## Journey 4: Young Person — From Discovery to Attending

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Trigger:** Friend brings them, parent registers them, school refers them, sees an event on Instagram.

### Stage 2: Pre-arrival anxiety
**Trigger:** Young person is told they're going to "athletics training."

**What the site can do:**
- Events page should feel welcoming to a young person browsing
- "All abilities. All welcome." messaging visible
- Photos of young people who look like them (Brent community)
- Coach Lumar story — relatable mentor who "found a new lane"

### Stage 3: First session
**Trigger:** Young person arrives at Willesden Sports Centre.

**Offline experience:** Staff welcome, age-appropriate group, introduced to coach and peers.

### Stage 4: Engagement
**Trigger:** Young person attends regularly, develops relationships.

### Stage 5: Progression
**Trigger:** Wants to compete, join a life skills workshop, or take on leadership.

**What the site does:**
- `/competitions` clear for them to see fixtures and entry process
- `/education` shows the life skills they can develop
- Performance coaching page for ambitious athletes
- Jack Petchey awards to celebrate achievements (visible on /impact)
