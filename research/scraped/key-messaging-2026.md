## Vision & Mission

- **Vision:** A world where every young person, regardless of background, has the opportunity, support, and belief to achieve their full potential — on the track, in the classroom and in life.
- **Mission:** We blend athletics, education, and mentoring to create a launchpad for young people to break down barriers, unlock their potential and thrive.

## The Problem (The Need in Brent)

Based in Brent, we work with young people (aged 5-23) facing significant challenges:

- **Poverty:** Brent has a 41% child poverty rate, the highest in outer London. (Trust for London, 2021)
- **Lack of opportunity:** 1 in 3 Brent neighbourhoods are in the top 10% nationally for poverty rates, limiting access to safe spaces and enrichment activities. (Brent Council JSNA, 2022)
- **Wellbeing & safety:** Only 28% of local young people feel they have enough safe spaces to socialise, and 1 in 3 children are affected by bullying. (Brent Youth Parliament 2024)
- **Health inequality:** By age 14, 55% of girls no longer meet recommended activity levels, impacting their long-term mental and physical health. (This Girl Can)
- **Education inequality:** "Disadvantaged pupils were 19.1 months of learning behind their peers by the end of secondary school." — Education Policy Institute, Annual Report 2025
- **Employment:** "Employment rates are, on average, higher amongst people with higher levels of qualifications. In 2024, 90% of Londoners aged 16-64 with qualifications higher than A-levels were employed." — Trust for London

### The cycle (as framed by Track Academy)
- Children from low-income families fall behind at school → they are twice as likely to end up unemployed as adults → poverty becomes an inheritance
- Education support catches them up → they gain qualifications and get good jobs → the cycle breaks

### The trusted adult evidence
- Young people from low-income families are twice as likely to lack emotional support at home (UK Millennium Cohort Study, 2024)
- "Having a trusted adult in childhood substantially mitigates the negative impacts of adversity on adult mental health and health-harming behaviours." — Bellis et al, BMC Psychiatry, 2017
- Problem: Poverty robs young people of consistent support at home → they become disconnected from school, training and work → the cycle continues
- Solution: Trusted adult relationships catch them → build confidence and keep them engaged → life trajectories change
- Young people facing adversity without a trusted adult are 32 times more likely to experience poor mental health and harmful behaviours in adulthood

## How We Solve It (Our Approach & Audience)

- **Who we support:** Young people aged **5-23** in under-resourced NW London communities. Our youth are **45% low-income families**, **85% ethnically diverse** and **63% girls/young women**.
- **What we do:** Track Academy is more than a sports club. We use athletics as the hook to deliver a holistic programme of education support, mentoring, life skills and holiday programmes. Our young people build transformative relationships that act as a network of support, helping young people build confidence, resilience and practical skills.

## The Change We Create (Our Impact)

### Outcome statement
We empower young people facing adversity to grow in confidence, resilience and education while building supportive networks — equipping them with the tools to shape their own futures.

### The proof points
- **The launchpad:** 90% of our participants leave us with the resilience, practical skills and a trusted network needed to navigate their education, careers, and communities.
- **Unshakeable resilience:** 80% of our young people build critical life skills and the confidence to tackle setbacks on their own terms.
- **The author of their story:** Over 90% of our young people believe they are the authors of their own futures, equipped with the tools to shape the world around them.
