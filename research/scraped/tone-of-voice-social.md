## Voice characteristics observed
1. **Warm, human, direct** — speaks to people, not at them
2. **Community-first** — always thanking, crediting, tagging partners
3. **Emotional but not saccharine** — celebrates without being cheesy
4. **Punchy** — short sentences, fragments for emphasis
5. **Inclusive language** — "All abilities. All welcome."
6. **Brand vocabulary:** "Bright Futures", "launchpad", "trusted adult", "sport for good", "Willesden vibes"
7. **Emoji use:** moderate (🔥, 🏅, 💙🧡, 🏃), not excessive
8. **Tags:** #trackacademy #brent #sportforgood #willesden #brightfutures
9. **Personal touch** — naming real people (Coach Lumar, Matthew, Yvonne, Luis Da Silva)
10. **Community shoutouts** — always crediting local businesses and partners
11. **Calls to action are clear but not pushy** — "Share, follow and watch this space"
12. **Humility + pride balance** — celebrates achievements without bragging

## What this tells us about the brand voice
- **Not corporate.** This is a grassroots, community-first charity.
- **Not childish.** Speaks to parents and adults seriously.
- **Proud but humble.** Celebrates achievements, credits the team.
- **Human.** Real people, real names, real relationships.
- **Hopeful.** "Bright Futures" isn't just a tagline — it runs through everything.
- **Brent-rooted.** Local identity matters — Willesden, Brent, NW10.
