# Track Academy — Safeguarding

**URL:** https://trackacademy.co.uk/safeguarding/

## Mission
At Track Academy, the safety, welfare and wellbeing of every child and young person is our highest priority. We are committed to providing a positive, safe and supportive environment where all athletes can train, develop and enjoy athletics free from harm, abuse or neglect.

We follow the safeguarding standards set by **England Athletics** and all our staff, coaches and volunteers are trained to recognise and respond to safeguarding concerns.

## Safeguarding Commitment
- Protecting children and young people from harm
- Providing safe, well-run training sessions and events
- Promoting respect, inclusion and positive behaviour
- Responding quickly and appropriately to any safeguarding concern

All staff and volunteers are subject to **DBS checks** and safeguarding training.

## What Is Safeguarding?
Protecting children and young people from:
- Physical, emotional or sexual abuse
- Neglect
- Bullying, harassment or discrimination
- Exploitation or inappropriate behaviour
- Online or social media risks

Applies to all activities: training sessions, competitions, trips, camps, online communication.

## How to Raise a Concern
1. Contact the Designated Safeguarding Lead
2. Provide as much information as possible (who, what, when, where)
3. Track Academy will take appropriate action (may contact England Athletics, social services, police)

All concerns handled confidentially, sensitively and seriously.
