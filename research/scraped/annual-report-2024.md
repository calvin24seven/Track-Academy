## Context — The Need

Systemic challenges in communities served:
- **Educational inequality:** Disparities in school funding, teacher shortages, overcrowded classrooms
- **Lack of youth spaces:** Only 28% of young people in Brent feel they have enough safe spaces to socialise (Brent Youth Parliament, 2023)
- **Mental health crisis:** One in three children in Brent are affected by bullying
- **Social isolation and distrust:** Just 41% of young people believe they can influence local decisions (Brent Youth Survey, 2023)
- **Gender inequality in sport:** By age 14, 64% of girls no longer meet recommended activity levels (Women in Sport, 2022)

## Impact Stats (2024)

### Core numbers
- **577** sessions across 52 weeks
- **331** young people, aged 5 to 21
- **64%** of participants are girls
- **85%** from ethnic minorities
- **85%** from communities across the borough of Brent
- **200+** competition entries

### Outcome metrics (self-reported by participants)
- **66%** improved school performance
- **87%** gained new skills for school and life
- **94%** believe they can achieve their goals
- **89%** feel more connected to their community
- **88%** believe Track Academy provides activities useful to them
- **89%** have made new friends since joining
- **76%** agree Track Academy improved their confidence
- **78%** said they could manage stress and setbacks well
- **89%** feel like they belong in their communities
- **83%** feel supported by teammates and coaches

### Education
- **120** attended Saturday school sessions (English, maths, science)
- New tutor Babatunde (specialises in science and maths) hired based on student demand
- Tesco grant of £5,000 for healthy breakfasts before Saturday school

### Life skills
- **279** young people attended at least one life skill session
- 10 core life skills: listening, presenting, networking/social skills, problem solving, creativity, staying positive, aiming high, discipline, team working, leadership
- Trustee Marion created a leadership session — Alaska survival scenario (used in current site content)

### Holiday programme
- **45** workshops to more than **250** students
- CV writing and interview skills workshop (ELEVATE Youth Mentoring Programme)
- Trips: laser tag, escape room

### Mentoring
- **3** new mentors recruited (Plamedie, Ngozi, Manar)
- Each mentor has 2-3 young people for weekly sessions
- **52** sessions between mentors and 12 mentees
- Mentoring dinners (new initiative 2024): 22 young women at Nando's with Yvonne, Ngozi, Plamedie; 8 male athletes dinner with Erick and Coach Lumar

### Girls in sport
- **215** girls and young women (one of largest cohorts yet)
- 47 aged 12-15
- Female-staffed environment
- Role models: Coach and former athlete Tayla Brade, alumni Annie Tagoe, icons Dina Asher-Smith, Keely Hodgkinson, Katarina Johnson-Thompson

## Mia Grey — Black History Month
Mia Grey shared thoughts with Sport England in a blog:
> "Track Academy's entire approach celebrates and amplifies black history and culture. Most of the staff, coaches, mentors, tutors and trustees reflect the demographic of people who attend."
> "Many of our student athletes are of Afro-Caribbean descent. Due to that, students are able to experience and learn more about black history through the staff, workshops and visitor athletes."
> "Food and music are the most basic ways that Track Academy integrates black culture and history into its events and workshops."
> "Brent is historically known for housing many of the Windrush generation, and immigrating cultures, so we've had the opportunity to work with a diaspora of student athletes who represent their culture."
> "Culture can be seen through the colloquial speech, traditions and norms in the community and weaving through the work."
> "Track Academy does all it can to encourage its athletes to strive for the best, by running life-skills workshops on aiming high, being creative, staying disciplined and positivity."
