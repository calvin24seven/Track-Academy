## Website Inspiration Analysis

For Track Academy, I would not look at generic web design galleries first. I'd look at organisations that already balance **young people, sport, education, mentoring, safeguarding, schools, impact, and fundraising**. The proposal already says the site needs to speak to **parents, schools and funders**, and the rebuild needs to reduce admin, improve credibility, create clearer journeys, rank in Google, and lay a foundation for future digital transformation.

### Best inspiration sites to study

#### 1. Greenhouse Sports — closest overall inspiration
This is probably the best reference for Track Academy.

**Why:** It combines sport + mentoring + schools + youth development + funders very well. Their homepage leads with a strong emotional mission, then explains the model, impact, donation route, parent/school info, safeguarding, and stories. Their navigation also has clear sections for parents, schools, young people, safeguarding, impact, partners, and donation.

**Steal:**
- "Every child deserves." style mission-led hero
- Clear explanation of sport + mentoring + trusted adult
- Parent/school/safeguarding routes
- Impact-led credibility
- Emotional case study quotes
- Support/donate journey

**Track Academy equivalent:** "Using athletics, mentoring and education to help young people build confidence, discipline and futures beyond the track."

#### 2. StreetGames — best for national-level impact and partner credibility
Strong because it makes the mission instantly understandable: changing young people's lives through sport. Homepage has clear routes for partners, young people, supporters and community partners, plus strong impact numbers.

**Steal:**
- Big impact stat section
- "How will you play your part?" audience routing
- Partner/funder credibility
- Campaign/news structure
- Strong "movement" language

**Track Academy equivalent:**
- Parents: Register your child
- Schools: Partner with us
- Funders: Back our impact
- Supporters: Donate / volunteer

#### 3. Dame Kelly Holmes Trust — best for athlete mentor positioning
Positions athletes as mentors and role models. "A Champion in your corner" is simple, emotional, easy to understand.

**Steal:**
- Mentor/role model storytelling
- Sport as a route to mindset and confidence
- Strong slogan-style positioning
- Partnership/fundraising journey
- Simple audience segmentation

**Track Academy equivalent:** "A coach, mentor and team behind every young person."

#### 4. Dallaglio RugbyWorks — best for sport + exclusion + long-term mentoring
Serves a similar "sport is the hook, mentoring is the transformation" model. Navigation: About, Our Work, Impact, Support, Events, News, Case Studies, Donate.

**Steal:**
- "Sport + long-term mentoring" framing
- Impact and case studies structure
- Events/fundraising routes
- "In their words" young person stories
- Clear support pathways

**Track Academy equivalent:** "Athletics opens the door. Mentoring, education and life skills change the journey."

#### 5. London Youth — best for institutional credibility and impact/data section
Nav: About, What We Do, Membership, Support Us, Impact and Data, News & Policy.

**Steal:**
- "Impact and data" as a top-level nav item
- Credible charity structure
- Strong funder-facing stats
- Programmes and support separated clearly
- Good model for annual reports/publications

**Track Academy equivalent top nav:** About | Programmes | Schools | Impact | Safeguarding | Events | Register | Support Us

#### 6. YoungMinds — best for parent/professional journey design
Immediately routes users by need: young person, parent, professional, support us. Strong urgent-help/safeguarding thinking, advice content, guides, real stories, reports/impact pages.

**Steal:**
- "Who are you?" user routing
- Parent guidance structure
- Professional/school resources
- Clear safeguarding-style trust pages
- Resource library / advice centre
- Human, reassuring language

**Track Academy equivalent:**
- I'm a parent
- I'm from a school
- I'm a funder/partner
- I want to register
- I want to support

#### 7. The King's Trust — best for scale and youth opportunity positioning
Worth reviewing for how a major youth charity frames confidence, courses, careers, support and outcomes.

**Steal:**
- Confidence/careers/opportunity language
- Young person journey design
- Programme discovery
- Partner/supporter credibility
- Outcome-led messaging

### The Best 3 to Use as Direct Design References
If you only pick three, use:
1. **Greenhouse Sports** — closest match for Track Academy
2. **StreetGames** — best impact/funder credibility
3. **YoungMinds** — best audience routing and parent/professional structure

Those three together give you: emotional mission, serious impact, parent trust, school clarity, and funder confidence.

### Suggested Track Academy Structure Based on These References

The homepage should not be a generic charity homepage. It should be built like this:

1. **Hero**
   - Clear mission
   - Strong young-person image
   - CTAs: Register / Partner with us / Support us

2. **Audience routes**
   - For Parents | For Schools | For Funders | For Young People

3. **What we do**
   - Athletics | Mentoring | Education & Life Skills | Performance Coaching | Holiday Programmes

4. **Why it matters**
   - Confidence | Discipline | School engagement | Wellbeing | Positive futures

5. **Impact numbers**
   - Young people supported | Schools/partners | Sessions delivered | Outcomes achieved

6. **Case study links**
   - Stories: Student stories | Parent quotes | School testimonials | Funder outcomes

7. **Trust**
   - Safeguarding | Team | Partners | Policies | Charity details

8. **Final CTA**
   - Register your child | Speak to us about school partnerships | Support our work

### Sites to also quickly review (secondary inspiration)
- **Chance to Shine** — school sport programme structure
- **Sported** — grassroots sport/funder ecosystem
- **Coach Core** — sport + young people + employability
- **Football Beyond Borders** — school mentoring, trusted adult, impact language
- **IntoUniversity** — education charity structure and school/funder credibility

### Design direction
Not corporate, not childish, not "sports club website." It should feel like a serious youth development charity that happens to use athletics as the engine.
